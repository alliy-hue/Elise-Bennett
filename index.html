Sound Familiar?

The 8 Signs Your Book Has a Discovery Problem, Not a Quality Problem

If even 2 or 3 of these feel familiar, your book almost certainly has a positioning gap that's blocking readers from finding it.

📉

"My reviews are great but nobody's finding the book"

You have a 4.0+ rating. Readers who find it love it. But your BSR is stuck past #500,000 and organic discovery has completely stalled. The quality is there. The visibility infrastructure is not.

🔇

"I launched and it just... disappeared"

You did the launch posts, told your friends, maybe ran a small promo. There was a brief spike, then nothing. Three months later, it feels like the book never existed. That's a launch strategy gap, not a book problem.

🎯

"I don't know who my readers are or where they gather"

You wrote the book, but you can't describe your ideal reader with precision. You don't know which categories they browse, which comp titles they love, or which communities they belong to. Without that map, marketing is just guessing.

📂

"I think I'm in the wrong categories but don't know which are right"

Amazon has 16,000+ categories. Being in the wrong 3 means your book is invisible to readers who would love it. This is the single most common fixable problem, and most authors don't even know it's happening.

📖

"Readers love Book 1 but never buy Book 2"

Your series has a read-through leak. Readers finish Book 1 and vanish. The cause is almost always a back-matter funnel gap: no next-book link, no newsletter CTA, or a Book 2 blurb that doesn't hook.

👻

"I have a backlist that's just sitting there doing nothing"

You published 2, 3, or 5+ books. The early ones stopped selling entirely. But those books still have value. They just need repositioning, refreshed metadata, and reintroduction to communities that have grown since they launched.

😰

"Marketing feels overwhelming and I don't know what actually works"

You've been told to do newsletters, BookTok, Instagram, blog tours, Amazon ads, and 47 other things. Nobody told you which actually matter for YOUR book in YOUR genre. You need a filter, not more noise.

🤷

"I'm spending money on marketing but can't tell if it's working"

You've tried ads, promos, or paid services. Maybe something helped, maybe not. You have no framework to evaluate what moved the needle. You need a data baseline, not more tactics.

FAQ

Questions Authors Ask Before Reaching Out

What's this "free assessment" actually include?+

I look at your book's publicly available data: Amazon BSR, category placement, keyword alignment, review patterns, also-bought carousel, pricing, and platform presence. Then I reply with honest observations about what I see and where the gaps are. It's a genuine analysis, not a sales pitch disguised as advice. If I can help, I'll explain how. If I can't, I'll say so.

Can you guarantee sales or bestseller status?+

No. Anyone who guarantees specific sales numbers or bestseller placement is not being honest with you. What I can guarantee is thorough research, honest analysis, data-backed strategy, and full transparency about what's realistic for your book in your market.

How is this different from other book marketing services?+

Most book marketers sell packages: social media posts, press releases, generic promotional templates. I start with your book's actual data. I look at your Amazon signals before I make any recommendations. Everything I suggest is based on observable market evidence specific to your book.

How far in advance should I reach out before launch?+

Ideally 60 to 90 days before publication. That gives us time to research your market, optimize your listing before it goes live, build your ARC strategy, and position everything correctly from day one. But I also work with backlist titles and books that have been out for years.

Do you work with self-published and traditionally published?+

Both. Self-published authors typically need help with the full spectrum of visibility infrastructure. Traditionally published authors often need help filling the marketing gap their publisher left behind after the initial 6-week push.

What if I'm introverted and hate self-promotion?+

Most of my clients feel this way. Good news: the most impactful changes have nothing to do with self-promotion. Category placement, keyword optimization, pricing strategy, series funnels, metadata improvements. These are infrastructure changes that work behind the scenes.

Do you manage Amazon or Facebook ads?+

Yes. But I never recommend ads until your listing is optimized first. Driving traffic to a poorly optimized listing is like pouring water into a leaky bucket. We fix the bucket first, then turn on the faucet.

How much does it cost?+

It depends on the scope of work your situation requires. After the free assessment, I'll recommend specific services and give you clear, upfront pricing with no hidden fees. I'm not the cheapest option available. I'm the option that bases everything on your actual data.

What's the difference between you and a publicist?+

A publicist focuses on media placement. I focus on the discoverability infrastructure that determines whether your book can be found by readers: Amazon positioning, metadata optimization, audience mapping, series funnels, and community strategy.

What do you need to get started?+

Your name, your book title, your genre, and your Amazon link (if published). That's it. I'll do the initial research with publicly available data and come back to you with what I see.
